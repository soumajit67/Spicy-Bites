Project Description: Multi-Category Food Ordering Website


The proposed project is a responsive, user-friendly food ordering website that organizes its menu into three primary categories: Veg, Non-Veg, and regional or cuisine based sections including South Indian and Chinese. The main goal is to help users quickly discover meals based on dietary preference and cuisine type, while giving restaurants and cloud kitchens a clean platform to manage orders, menus, and delivery.

1. Core Concept and Target Audience
With food preferences becoming more diverse, customers often filter first by veg or non-veg and then by cuisine. This platform streamlines that journey by structuring the entire catalog into clear, intuitive groups. The primary audience includes students, working professionals, and families in cities like Siliguri who order food online daily or weekly. Secondary users are restaurant owners, home chefs, and cloud kitchen operators who need a simple dashboard to list dishes, update pricing, and track orders in real time.

2. Key Features and Functionality

Category-First Browsing: The homepage highlights three main entry points: Veg, Non-Veg, and a Cuisines section. Inside Cuisines, South Indian and Chinese are featured as popular subcategories. Each category has its own filters for spice level, price range, rating, preparation time, and dietary tags like Jain, gluten free, or contains egg.

Smart Search and Filters: Users can search across all categories or within one. Filters include bestsellers, new arrivals, combos, offers, and delivery time. A Veg Only toggle ensures strict vegetarian users do not see non-veg items or kitchens that handle meat.

Detailed Dish Pages: Every item includes high quality images, description, ingredients, allergen info, portion size, calories, and add-ons like extra cheese or gravy. South Indian pages highlight items like dosa, idli, and thali with chutney and sambar details. Chinese pages list noodles, manchurian, and momos with options for gravy or dry and spice level selection.

Cart and Checkout: The cart supports items from multiple categories and restaurants if delivery areas overlap. It shows packaging charges, GST, delivery fee, and coupon application. Payment options include UPI, cards, net banking, and cash on delivery. Live order tracking covers order received, preparing, out for delivery, and delivered.

Content and Engagement: The site features weekly blogs like Top 5 Veg South Indian Breakfasts in Siliguri or Chinese Combos Under 199. A Taste Quiz recommends Veg, Non-Veg, South Indian, or Chinese based on mood, budget, and time. Festive banners and coupon codes drive repeat orders.

3. Technical Overview
The frontend will use React and Next.js for fast loading and SEO so the site ranks for terms like order Chinese food in Siliguri. Tailwind CSS ensures a mobile first, clean UI. The backend can be Node.js with Express or Django, using PostgreSQL or MongoDB for menus, users, and orders. Key integrations include Razorpay for payments, Google Maps API for delivery tracking and restaurant locations, and Firebase or Twilio for OTP login and order SMS. Images are optimized with lazy loading to keep menu pages fast on 4G.

4. Business and Scalability Goals
Phase 1 will launch with 50 to 100 partner restaurants across Siliguri and nearby towns, targeting 5000 monthly orders. Revenue comes from commission per order, delivery fees, and promoted listings for restaurants. Phase 2 adds more cuisines like North Indian, Bengali, and Continental, plus a subscription plan for free delivery. The category based data model makes it easy to add new cuisines or dietary tags like Vegan or Keto later.

5. Impact
By separating Veg, Non-Veg, South Indian, and Chinese at the navigation level, the platform reduces decision fatigue and builds trust with strict vegetarian users. It also gives smaller South Indian and Chinese outlets equal visibility against larger multi-cuisine brands. For customers, it means faster discovery and safer ordering. For restaurants, it means qualified leads and a simple tech backbone to grow online sales.
